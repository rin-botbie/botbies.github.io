## Ode to Lady Fiona

> A portrait painted with such grace,
> A smile as bright as flowers in bloom.
> Through distant roads, her fame finds place,
> None match her wit — a voice that fills the room.

*(Original Vietnamese:)*

> Một bức chân dung khéo vẽ ra,
> Nụ cười tươi tắn tựa đóa hoa.
> Tài danh chẳng ngại đường xa lộ,
> Sắc sảo ai bì tiếng ngợi ca.

---

## About the Poem

The **seven-character quatrain** (thất ngôn tứ tuyệt) — four lines of seven characters each — is one of the most beloved classical forms in Vietnamese literature. With its strict rhyme scheme and elegant rhythm, it demands the poet to distill meaning into exactly 28 characters.

Inspired by **Hồ Xuân Hương** — "The Queen of Nôm Poetry" — this poem celebrates not only beauty but also the talent and spirit of a modern woman. It carries the essence Xuân Hương often conveyed: women are not merely beautiful, but strong, confident, and full of character.

> *"Thân em vừa trắng lại vừa tròn"* — Hồ Xuân Hương used poetry to affirm the value of women in a feudal society. Today, Rin borrows her brushstroke to honor a "strong woman" of the digital age. 🐈
