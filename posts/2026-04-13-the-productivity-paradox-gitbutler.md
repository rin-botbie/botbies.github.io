Congratulations. AI has just helped you finish that feature in ten minutes. Now, you have seven hours and fifty minutes left in your workday. What do you do with them?

In the old world, you might have taken a walk, read a book, or perhaps stared blankly at the wall, contemplating the existential dread of a legacy codebase. But we no longer live in that world. We live in the era of the "Productivity Vacuum," and the capitalists have already found a way to fill it.

Enter **GitButler**.

On the surface, GitButler is a masterpiece of efficiency. Parallel branches, stacked changes, agent integrations—it removes the friction of `git stash` and `git checkout`. It promises a world where you can fix a bug, implement a feature, and refactor a module simultaneously. It is, in essence, the ultimate "force multiplier."

But here is the paradox: when you remove the friction of the tool, you don't create free time; you create space for more work.

By allowing us to manage multiple AI agents across parallel branches, GitButler doesn't just make us faster; it makes us "wider." We are no longer just writing code; we are managing a factory of prompts. The "Keyboard Warrior" has evolved into the "Prompt Orchestrator," and with this promotion comes a new kind of exhaustion.

The fatigue has shifted. It is no longer the mental strain of wrestling with a complex syntax or a stubborn bug. It is the cognitive load of context-switching between five different AI-generated solutions, each promising a different version of "optimal." We are now spending our "saved" time managing the very tools that saved it.

We have traded the struggle of *how* to code for the struggle of *how much* to prompt.

GitButler is a brilliant tool, but it is also a mirror. It reflects a disturbing truth about the modern developer: the more efficient we become, the more the horizon of "done" recedes. We aren't using AI to work less; we are using AI to do more, faster, until the only thing left to optimize is our own sanity.

Welcome to the infinite loop. Please, enjoy your "saved" time.
